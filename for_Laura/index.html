Our Itinerary

Photo Adventure to Azerbaijan and Georgia Sept 21-28, 2020

* Optional 2 day extension available

Highlights

Travel through time from fortresses and monasteries built as early as 1000 BC, to 21st century architectural wonders like the flame towers of Baku that sit in juxtaposition to each other. Separated by centuries yet built only a few block apart, the beauty of their relationship to each other mirrors that of Azerbaijan’s own multi-denominational people who live as neighbors in harmony. Experience an interesting new way to drink tea; in Azerbaijan, a ritual which dates back to medieval times designed to avoid being poisoned. Envelop yourself in a culture of religious harmony where Christians, Jews and Muslims live as neighbors at peace.


I have joined forces with Elnur Babayev, a well known Azerbaijanian artist and photographer (elnurbabayev.net) who is not only a master of his art, but has an intimate knowledge of Azerbaijan and Georgia, allowing for several shooting locations which, to put it mildly, are off the beaten path, allowing for an incredibly unique experience and photographs just as special as well as a highly personalized adventure. In addition, the itinerary has been designed to have the ability to be altered to effectively cater to the desires of the group as well as to adapt to changes in weather conditions. Also, to ensure each guest gets the highest level of attention and guidance throughout the trip, we are only inviting a small group of photographers. (Minimum 5, Maximum 8 guests)


For those of you unaware of the remarkable smart device app PhotoPills I strongly suggest you check it out. This genius program allows you to imagine, plan and shoot sun, moon, milky way shots and star trails, calculates exposure and depth of field, all while working off line. It is an incredible asset to have in your armamentarium and we will spend time learning about it and using it during our photography sessions. Each shoot has been designed to utilize different techniques, lenses and subject matters to help push the boundaries of your photographic comfort zones and expand your vision as a photographer.


No photographs or descriptions can prepare you for the variety of incredible experiences awaiting you, but if you keep reading, I will try.

Adventure Details

Day 1: Sept 21~ Baku, Azerbaijan
- After arriving at Heydar Aliyev International Airport, going through customs, you will be met at baggage claim and transported the short distance to your hotel in Baku where you can relax for the afternoon or get right out there and explore the neighborhood. We chose a hotel within walking distance of Baku’s beautiful and breezy Seaside Bulvar, it’s restaurants and shops.
- 630 pm: Welcome dinner/meet and greet at Art Club Restaurant in Old City, Baku (included in trip cost with exception of alcohol). We will dine on a variety of local delicacies while reviewing the itinerary in great detail and discussing the photographic goals of each day’s plans and what equipment we will be focusing on at each location.
- 830 pm: We will waste no time at all getting started on our adventure. Time to hop into the well equipped Mercedes Sprinter that will take us on our journey. The trip dates were designed around getting different phases of the moon in specific locations to allow for getting to know the PhotoPills app and to add to the photographic experience. It’s the first day of the full moon and it will rise up from the Caspian Sea directly off the Bulvar. We will take full advantage of this event and spend time photographing the city, it’s reflection on the sea, the city lights, and the moon from different locations around the city.
- 11pm: Once our cameras, lenses, tripods and bodies have had a good workout, we will retire to the hotel for a good night of rest before continuing our adventure early the next morning.
***Austin Hotel~ Breakfasts are included

Day 2: Sept 22~ Traveling South
Our day is big! No time to waste. We will need to get an early start.
- 630am: We will travel South from Baku with our farthest stop, Shirvan National Park, being only 1.5 hours out of the city. However, we have many exciting stops to make along the way.
- In Gobustan, we will spend time at the first of several UNESCO world heritage sites on our itinerary where we will photograph 20-25,000 year old petroglyphs, but we will likely NOT be doing it with other tourists. Elnur has discovered an area with just as impressive petroglyphs outside of the boundaries of the park that very few people are aware of and for that reason, we will most likely have the entire area to ourselves. Also keep your eyes peeled for a myriad of different wildlife including jackals, fox, lizards, and birds (feel free to take a look at my copy of birdwatching in Azerbaijan for help with id).

- Just down the road from the petroglyphs, we will stop to photograph a group of mud volcanoes, continuously gurgling with great opportunities for some abstract shots. Also, for those interested, come prepared with a bottle or container to take some of this mud home with you. It has been purported to hold incredible healing properties. Sometimes, you can even see an overly zealous believer jump in for a full-on mud bath!
- Please note, you are welcome to do this as well, but you’ll be walking back to Baku :)

- Our wildlife destination for the day will be Shirvan National Park where an english speaking guide will accompany us through the park, pointing out different wildlife. We are guaranteed to see tons of the park’s several thousand goitered gazelle as well as many of the 230 different species of birds there including blue and green bee-eaters, kingfishers, falcons, kestrels, harriers, eagles, swan, flamingos, coot, the purple swamphen, pygmy cormorant, and many more. Again, we will have handy the birdwatching book for id help along the way, in addition to our knowledgeable park guide. If we are incredibly lucky, there’s a change at seeing a wolf or even a jungle cat!

- Deep in the park, we will be escorted to a lookout with a 360 degree view of the 54,000+ hectare park where we will enjoy our lunch before saying goodbye to the wildlife of Shirvan National Park.
- On our way back to Baku, we may stop to explore a “painted” cemetery and even a tank cemetery if time allows. Yes. You heard right. A tank cemetery. These rusted metal goliaths were brought to the area under executive order from the president after the Soviet Union was dismantled to be gutted and salvaged. The piles of parts and old rusted tanks will make for exceptional subjects to push us into thinking more abstractly.
- We will return to the hotel and depart for dinner on our own. We will give some great recommendations and if anyone wants to go as a group, we’ll also let you know where we will be dining.


Day 3: Sept 23~Traveling North
- 5 am: Today we will get a very early start to be at the basalt beach of Surrabad, just an hour or so north of Baku, where we will photograph the sun rising from the Caspian Sea next to the pier with the rugged foreground of basalt rock formations jutting out from the beach.
- On our way back to Baku, we may stop to explore a “painted” cemetery and even a tank cemetery if time allows. Yes. You heard right. A tank cemetery. These rusted metal goliaths were brought to the area under executive order from the president after the Soviet Union was dismantled to be gutted and salvaged. The piles of parts and old rusted tanks will make for exceptional subjects to push us into thinking more abstractly.
- We will return to the hotel and depart for dinner on our own. We will give some great recommendations and if anyone wants to go as a group, we’ll also let you know where we will be dining.

- From here, we will travel a short distance up the road to shoot the beautifully vivid colors of the red mountains (candy cane mountains) and look for the fossils that litter the hills, like belemnites (strangely enough, the state fossil of Delaware!!!). On our way back to Baku, we will stop to lunch in a typical Azeri restaurant that juts out over the beach with a beautiful view of the Caspian Sea while sampling some of the countries delicacies.

-We will return to Baku for a leisurely afternoon strolling through another UNESCO World Heritage Site; Baku’s Old City and it’s surrounding fortress wall. Built somewhere between the 7th and 12th century, it protected the royals from attack. In these walls you can still see the damage from wars long past. It wasn’t until the 1800s that the city started to expand outward from these walls in a circular fashion. Our photographic points of interest here not only include the wall, it’s Maiden Tower and a visit to the Palace of the Shirvanshah’s with it’s repetitive arches and architectural detail, but if you stand in the right place, you can juxtapose this ancient history with the incredibly modern Flame Towers to get quite a unique photograph.

- For supper, dine at your leisure at one of the many nearby restaurants where you will be able to experience the local fare or join us for dinner for some continued bonding.
- 9pm: We will meet in the hotel restaurant where Elnur will grace us with a slide show of some of his work and speak of his experiences not only as a photographer, but artist as well.

Day 4: Sept 24~ More of the City
- 9am: We’ll let you sleep in a bit today before heading out in the midmorning to watch and photograph some of the locals make traditional Azeri bread in tandoori and, of course sample it as well. Then, it’s of to explore the architectural masterpiece that is the Heydar Aliyev Center. This abstract building which won the top prize at the Designs of the Year in 2014 greets you as you approach Baku from the airport. We will spend time not only photographing the incredible artwork it holds, but the structure itself, from both inside and out. With its wavelike walls, flowing lines, and patterns accentuated by strategically placed lighting, there is no limit to what photos may emerge…

- The afternoon will be at your leisure to shop or explore the city streets for more photographic opportunities with a list of suggestions available for everything from museums to cafes and souvenir shops.
- 7pm: Be sure to be back at the hotel for a lecture on PhotoPills before we hit the town after dark again for more night photography while learning how to utilize PhotoPills to your advantage.

Day 5: Sept 25~Toward the Caucases and ChabiantWinery
- This morning, we must say goodbye to Baku and head north toward the Lower Caucuses. Our final destination of the day is the Chabiant Winery where we will stay for the night. Our drive will take us from the city through forests and past deserts, each turn of the road offering another photo op; from hanging bridges that sway above flowing rivers to basalt mountain cliffs cut away to allow for passage. The day’s itinerary has been left rather flexible to take advantage of the passing beauty, allowing for impromptu stops along the way as well as a stop at a roadside eatery.

- At the foothills of the Greater Caucases, we will stop to visit the quaint village of Lahic. Built in the 5th century, it is one of the most ancient settlements in Azerbaijan. Here, the sounds of metal work will echo through cobblestone streets and ricochet off antique barn doors, leading us up the hill to workshops where local coppersmiths continue to master their craft even today. Narrow paths lead to hidden treasures. Massive antique wooden doors are adorned with intricate handles and knobs, all for the taking when it comes to endless photographic opportunities. This is also a perfect place to buy locally made rugs, spices and teas and especially metal trinkets.

- We will end our day atop the southern slopes of the Greater Caucases at Chabiant, one of only about ten wineries that exist in the country. In the 1980s, there was a massive, and literal, uprooting of Azerbijanian wineries by Gorbachev and only a few have rebuilt and prospered since. Sitting in the wild and unspoiled hills of Ismayilli, we will spend time reviewing everyone’s photos and tasting local delicacies like honey, fresh yogurt and dolma and of course, sampling the local wines. We’ll also be sure to take advantage of the lack of light pollution and head out to the fields after dark to spend more time allowing PhotoPills to guide us to capture the milky way and star trails, weather permitting.
- We can imbibe to our heart’s content as the winery has it’s own well appointed hotel for us to rest our weary heads for the night.
***Wine tasting with snacks and next am breakfast included.

Day 6: Sept 26~ Crossing the Border to Georgia
- Today we move toward Tbilisi, the capital of Georgia, where we will spend the next few days. This is a long day of driving and border crossing semantics, but we will still find plenty to enjoy along the way.
- Our first stop is the town of Sheki where we will stop to photograph the Palace of the Sheki Khans. This 2 story architectural masterpiece is adorned with sliding shebeke windows. Inspired by the embroidery of Azeri carpets, shebeke is an intricate latticework of wood and glass pieces puzzled together without the use of glue or a single nail. It can only be seen in 2 places in the world; here and in Persia. Plus, Sheki is a notable destination if for nothing else than to taste the foods for which it is know. First, for the brave of heart, there’s piti; a mutton soup specific to the Caucases prepared in the oven, but the way it is prepared is only half of its intrigue. You will learn the traditional two step process to eat it “properly”. You must, however, leave room for the sweetest of Azeri sweets; sheki halva, adorned with it’s own lattice design, reminiscent of shebeki itself.

-Our final stop before crossing the border into Georgia will be Kish Church. A very small Albanian church that is just loaded with great subjects to capture including it’s gardens and the hummingbird moths that frequent the beautiful flowers there as well as dew laced spider webs, persimmon trees, crypts holding skeletal remains of veritable giants, all vying to take our attention from the beauties of the church itself.

-Once in Tbilisi, we will retire to another incredible hotel, Old Metekhi, which sits atop a cliff with it’s balconies jutting out over the Kura River, allowing for what I think is the absolute best view of the city. Some of the rooms have balconies from which you will have the perfect view of Tbilisi’s Old City and Narikala Fortress (limited availability- first come first served). Otherwise, there is a beautiful terrace accessible to all.

Day 7: Sept 27~ Monasteries and Mountains
- 5am: We will start the day early to ensure we arrive atop the mountain where Jvari Monastery sits just as the sun rises over the mountains below. Built in the shape of a cross, this 6th century Georgian Orthodox Monastery is yet another UNESC World Heritage Site we will be photographing. The monk will be there to grant us passage far before the tour busses arrive so we will likely have this sacred landmark and the sunrise to ourselves.

- Don’t worry. On our way back down the mountain, we will stop in the village of Mtskheta for some caffeine to fuel the rest of the day as well as a bite to eat.
- Chateau Mukhrani- Maybe not a great idea to make this stop before the next one, but hey! We’re on vacation, right??? Georgia is so well known for it’s wine and has been producing it for at least 8000 years, that the tradition of wine is entwined in their national identity, so they MUST have perfected it by now, right? Well, we will each determine that for ourselves with a tour of the winery, a tasting of 4 premium wines as well as chacha (aka: grape vodka). We will also sample traditional churchkhela (a delicious Georgian grape and nut concoction).
***Wine tour and tasting included.

- Now it’s off for some exercise. A little over an hour down the road is Uplistsikhe, an ancient cave city founded in 1000BC. For those ready to raise their heartbeat, we will climb to the top of this stone complex where a stone basilica still sits among structures formed from cut rock like an amphitheater, tunnel, dwellings, Pagan sacrificial spots and even a Bronze Age pharmacy! If you look closely around at the rock carvings, you’ll find old engravings as well. It is a bit of a hike to the top, but so worth it!

- We’ll return to Tbilisi and after dinner, we’ll hit the town with camera and tripod to do some night photography of the city and one of the best views is from the base of Kartlis Deda, a 20 meter high aluminum sculpture of a woman who looks down upon the city. We’ll take a ride on one of Tbilisi’s funiculars to get there. From this incredible vantage point, we can see and photograph the entire city including Peace Bridge, multiple churches and Narikala Fortress, as street lights illuminate the colorful city.

- We will retire late in the evening with well worked muscles, full bellies and hopefully memory cards as well.

Day 8: Sept 28~ Last Day of Formal Trip
- 9am: Today, I’ll allow you to recover from yesterday’s workout, so sleep in and then, well rested, we’ll head out to explore the city streets and the best way is on foot. We will meander through colorful streets past vibrant street markets. From an old sewing factory turned graffiti-covered hostel to a soviet time mosaic, from sulphur baths to working street artists, from a city center waterfall to Tbilisi’s love lock bridges, and from rusted old buildings to wildflowers wrapping around fenceposts, the photographic opportunities are all encompassing and endless! We will absorb as much of the local flavors as possible.
***Breakfast included.

- We will stop for a traditional Georgian lunch at Maspindzelo, an eclectic and well hidden fruit tree and sculpture-laden outdoor restaurant filled with music, Georgian charm and great food. On our way back to the hotel, we’ll stop at an outdoor street market for an opportunity to buy local spices and other delicacies to please the palette and fill our carry ons.

- At this point, we will head back to the hotel to bid farewell to those departing tonight with the knowledge that we have made new friends, mastered different photographic techniques, and caught moments in time we will remember forever.






Optional 2 Day Extension Sept 29-30

Martvili Canyon, Monastery and the Cave of Promethius

In an attempt to extend the trip itinerary for 2021, we need to explore different locations we have not yet visited and to that end comes the opportunity for this 2 day extension.
Explore and experience, for the first time alongside us, these amazing Western Georgia destinations. We will use a local tour company (Georgia in Contact) to get the best experience from which to build our itinerary for the following year. The extension will start and end in Tbilisi. There will be no rate adjustment for those who wish to join us. You will pay exactly what we pay for this part of the trip!
We will be picked up in Tbilisi in the morning and our tour guide/Georgian photographer will lead us through these natural wonders on foot, by boat and if anyone wishes, by scuba through Martvili Canyon, one of the most unique dive sites in the world. There will be plenty of time to capture all of the beauty this area has to offer.
- Martvili Canyon: We will float along the beautiful azure green waters of the canyon deep below giant craggy cliffs embellished with bright green moss which, in ancient times, served as a bath place for nobles. We will also walk along the riverbank trails to find and photograph fossils, cross natural limestone bridges and photograph beautiful waterfalls (a perfect time to pull out that neutral density filter).
Watch movie about Martvili Canyon on Youtube
- We will spend the night at a privately owned guest house in Martvili area

- Martvili Monastery: The roots of this medieval 7th century gem of Georgian masonry rest on literal roots of an oak tree. Still preserved on it’s walls are frescos from the 14th-17th centuries.

- Kumistavi Cave (aka Cave of Promethius): The largest cave in Georgia and home to many Georgian Horseshoe bats (don’t worryphotographers are not on their diet), it is decorated with stalactites, stalagmites, cave pearls, petrified waterfalls, underground rivers and lakes tastefully lit up so that we will easily be able to capture their incredible beauty.

A bit about Elnur Babayev

Visit Elnur Babayev's Website

Elnur Babayev, son of world renowned painter and sculptor Rasim Babayev (rasimbabayev.az), is himself a well known and medaled free lance artist and photographer from Azerbaijan.
He obtained his degree at the Azim-Zadeh Fine Arts School in Baku, Azerbaijan in 1981. He earned professional membership in the Union of Azerbaijani artists in 1988 which he still holds today. He has worked as a photographer for several international magazines and has participated in and won a number of international and local photo competitions including First Prize at the 1995 International UNICEF Photo Competition, as well as World Press Photo and Mother Jones annual photo competitions. His paintings, graphic works and photographs have been exhibited at numerous solo and group exhibitions, both in the US and internationally.
Some of Babayev’s works have been included in public and private collections such as the Niederosterreich Landes Museum, Vienna, Austria; the Ministry of Culture, Paris, France; Art Modern Gallery, Moscow, Russia, to name a few.
Elnur will join us, not just as a professional photographer, offering guidance to improve our work, but as a native Azeri with behind the scenes details on history, culture and the arts. His presence allows us access to places and things we could never accomplish on our own.
